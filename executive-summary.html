Executive Summary

Price Check

Turning $3B in transaction data into a free, self-serve pricing tool for SaaS buyers

Hannah Garner

Product Manager, Vendr

1
Company

From service to product-led growth

Vendr helped companies buy software through a concierge service. The result: $3B+ in real transaction data across thousands of SaaS vendors. The challenge: that data only served existing customers.

$3B+ Transaction data
5,000+ Companies served
20,000+ SaaS products covered

The opportunity: unlock that data for every buyer, not just paying customers.

2
Problem

SaaS buyers have no idea if they're getting a fair price

80%+ of buyers research pricing before talking to a rep. But there's nowhere to get an honest, data-backed answer. They're Googling, asking peers, guessing.

No transparency SaaS pricing is opaque by design. Vendors don't publish real prices. Every deal is "custom."
No benchmark Buyers have no way to know if a $400K Salesforce contract is above or below market.
3
Hypothesis

Give the data away for free. Let every use make it smarter.

If we let any buyer upload a contract and get an instant, data-backed price assessment — free, self-serve, no rep required
Then we build trust with buyers before they enter the sales funnel, grow top-of-funnel, and make the pricing model more accurate with every upload
Because every contract uploaded enriches the dataset. The product gets smarter with every use — a compounding data moat.
4
The Team

Cross-functional, tightly coupled

I led product strategy, discovery, and delivery — working directly with a small, focused team that shipped together every week.

Product Strategy, roadmap, instrumentation, and customer signal
Engineering Upload pipeline, AI summary engine, sharing infrastructure
Data Science Pricing model accuracy, degradation tiers, coverage monitoring
Content Result presentation, trust signals, buyer-facing language

Shared instrumentation meant the whole team could see what was working — no handoffs, no waiting for reports.

5
Strategy

Ship small, learn fast, compound

Four releases in three months. Each one shipped what we learned from the last — and created new signal for the next.

01
Concierge MVP Manually assembled PDF from back-office data. No automation. Validated that buyers would use it.
02
In-product release Shipped to existing customers. Learned which data buyers actually used. Instrumented everything.
03
Public launch Opened to anyone. Tested self-serve demand. Refined extraction across new document types.
04
AI + sharing Added AI summaries, no-auth sharing, tightened degradation tiers. Killed features below the bar.
6
The Product

Upload a contract. Get a fair price.

From back-office data to an instant answer: bell curve pricing, AI summary, competitor alternatives, and a clear assessment — all in under a minute.

7
Challenges

What we had to navigate

CEO pressure to launch big "Why not all 20,000 products on day one?" Used data to show thin coverage eroded trust. Held the line: top suppliers first.
Data degradation Vendor pricing changed faster than our data refreshed. Built monitoring to flag thinning coverage before it hit users.
AI accuracy gaps Summaries sometimes missed contract nuance. Below-3-star ratings triggered personal follow-ups to understand what fell short.
8
Customer Outcomes

Am I getting a fair price?

That's the question we set out to answer.

Under 1 min Contract upload to "am I overpaying?" answered
4.5-star avg In-product rating — below 3 stars triggered a personal follow-up
$90M/month AI-powered pricing recommendations on live contracts

Price Check fed a marketplace that crossed $1B in annual volume — serving 5,000+ companies buying software fair, fast, and easy.

The process: start with a concierge experiment, instrument everything, ship small coupled releases, and let real usage tell you what to build next.
