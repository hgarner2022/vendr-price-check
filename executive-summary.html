Portfolio Case Study

Price Check

Turning $3B in locked transaction data into a free pricing tool for every SaaS buyer

Hannah Garner · Staff Product Manager

1
Company

From service to product-led growth

Vendr helped companies buy software through a concierge service. The result: $3B+ in real transaction data across thousands of SaaS vendors. The challenge: that data only served existing customers.

$3B+ Transaction data
5,000+ Companies served
20,000+ SaaS products covered

The opportunity: unlock that data for every buyer, not just paying customers.

2
Problem

SaaS buyers have no idea if they're getting a fair price

80%+ of buyers research pricing before talking to a rep. But there's nowhere to get an honest, data-backed answer. They're Googling, asking peers, guessing.

No transparency SaaS pricing is opaque by design. Vendors don't publish real prices. Every deal is "custom."
No benchmark Buyers have no way to know if a $400K Salesforce contract is above or below market.
3
Who we’re building for

“We’re about to sign and I have
no idea if this price is fair.”

The department head They buy software for their teams — but it’s not their full-time job. IT directors, heads of finance, marketing leads. Smart, busy, and making high-stakes purchasing decisions with almost no pricing data.
They have a quote — but no benchmark

They’ve been through the sales cycle. A contract is in front of them. But there’s no public data to tell them if the number is fair.

The information asymmetry is massive

Vendors negotiate these deals daily across hundreds of clients. Buyers do it once every few years. 60% of vendors deliberately mask price increases.

Overpaying is the default

80%+ of companies overpay for software. Without benchmarks, you’re likely paying 20–30% above market — and you’d never know it.

They don’t need a procurement team. They need confidence — an instant, data-backed answer before they sign.

4
Hypothesis

Give the data away for free. Let every use make it smarter.

If we let any buyer upload a contract and get an instant, data-backed price assessment — free, self-serve, no rep required
Then we build trust with buyers before they enter the sales funnel, grow top-of-funnel, and make the pricing model more accurate with every upload
Because every contract uploaded enriches the dataset. The product gets smarter with every use — a compounding data moat.

6
Strategy

Ship small, learn fast, compound

Four releases in three months. Each one shipped what we learned from the last — and created new signal for the next.

01
Concierge MVP Manually assembled PDF from back-office data. No automation. Validated that buyers would use it.
02
In-product release Shipped to existing customers. Learned which data buyers actually used. Instrumented everything.
03
Public launch Opened to anyone. Tested self-serve demand. Refined extraction across new document types.
04
AI + sharing Added AI summaries, no-auth sharing, tightened degradation tiers. Killed features below the bar.
7
The Product

Upload a contract. Get a fair price.

From back-office data to an instant answer: bell curve pricing, AI summary, competitor alternatives, and a clear assessment — all in under a minute.

8
Challenges

What we had to navigate

01
Data coverage gaps

Didn’t have data on every vendor. Designed graceful degradation — full SKU benchmarks where we had depth, negotiation insights where we didn’t.

02
Misaligned customer expectations

Surfacing every supplier as a “price check” set the wrong expectation. When we couldn’t deliver an actual price comparison — just negotiation insights — customers gave us sub-3-star ratings. We had to rethink how we framed what we could deliver.

03
Extraction accuracy (evals)

Getting Extend.ai to 95%+ across 10+ doc types required iterating on prompts, tuning edge cases, and building eval pipelines.

04
Normalizing messy pricing data

Every vendor names products differently — “seats” vs. “licenses,” hidden bundle costs, line items that change between customers. AI can read it, but comparing prices means an intelligence layer that understands what the data means.

Coverage tiers — from full SKU data to negotiation-only insights
