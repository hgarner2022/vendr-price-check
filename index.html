Portfolio Case Study

Price Check

Unlocking $3B in transaction data to help buyers save on SaaS

Hannah Garner · Staff Product Manager

01
Company

From service to product-led growth

Vendr sat alongside buyers and helped them negotiate their software contracts. The result: $3B+ in real transaction data across thousands of SaaS vendors. The challenge we kept hearing from customers: how do we get this data into their hands — and expose it to the broader ecosystem?

$3B+ Transaction data
5,000+ Companies served
20,000+ SaaS products covered

The opportunity: put that data directly in every buyer’s hands.

02
Problem Definition

SaaS pricing is completely opaque

$900B spent on software in 2023
250+ SaaS products per company
20–25% overpaid on average

Not one of them publishes what it actually costs. There are no benchmarks, no comparables, no way to know if the number on the table is fair.

Big companies have procurement teams, pricing data, and negotiation experts to make sure they never overpay. Most companies don’t. They’re left guessing — or hoping they got a “good deal.”

03
Company Strategy

Pricing data everywhere

Vendr’s product roadmap centered on three pillars — build the best pricing AI, open it through public APIs, and embed insights in every software buying journey.

Pricing AI

Analyzes hundreds of thousands of software quotes to build multi-dimensional fair price profiles — aware of term length, usage quantities, and purchase type.

Public APIs

Enables partners — marketplaces, review sites, consulting firms — to embed Vendr’s pricing insights in their own products and experiences.

Experiences

Pricing insights embedded directly in procurement journeys — the Vendr website, Chrome extension, buyer guides, and Price Check.

I owned the Experiences layer — the right data, to the right person, at the right time.

Let every use make it smarter.

Buyers weren’t going to come to us — we needed to go to them. Price Check was my bet: make the richest pricing dataset in SaaS free, instant, and self-serve. Every upload feeds the flywheel — more data in, smarter answers out.

< 1 min Upload to pricing insight
20,000+ Products with pricing data
100% free Self-serve, no sales team required

Building the Product

The foundation was already there

For six months, our team had been building an internal document processing tool with Extend.ai — extracting structured pricing data from the contracts and quotes flowing through Vendr every day.

Tens of millions of pages — contracts, quotes, order forms, renewal notices

Extraction at scale

Extend.ai processed 10+ document types at 95%+ accuracy — from 30-page legal agreements to complex order forms with nested tables.

Schema & normalization

SKU mapping, pricing tiers, and scoring models turned raw contract data into structured, comparable intelligence.

Locked behind services

Analysts used this tool internally to prep data for negotiations. Powerful, but only accessible through our services team.

I led the Extend.ai integration — iterating on extraction prompts, tuning models against edge cases, and defining the accuracy thresholds. This became the intelligence backbone for everything we built next.

08

The data lived in our back office

Handcrafted PDFs · delivered in-app · 48–72 hr turnaround

I set the discovery strategy — sitting in on live procurement calls, running weekly sessions with our SaaS buying specialists, and interviewing customers to understand what they actually needed:

What did people want?

Pricing benchmarks, not product reviews. Fair price, not feature comparison.

What was important?

Speed, specificity, and trust in the numbers. Buyers needed confidence before a negotiation.

What were we learning?

Every handcrafted report taught us what to extract, what to score, and what to automate.

A deliberate experiment: ~20 handcrafted reports a day, each one teaching us what to extract, what to score, and what customers actually valued — before we automated a single thing.

11
Challenges

What we had to navigate

01
Data coverage gaps

Didn’t have data on every vendor. Designed graceful degradation — full SKU benchmarks where we had depth, negotiation insights where we didn’t.

02
Misaligned customer expectations

Surfacing every supplier as a “price check” set the wrong expectation. When we couldn’t deliver an actual price comparison — just negotiation insights — customers gave us sub-3-star ratings. We had to rethink how we framed what we could deliver.

03
Extraction accuracy (evals)

Getting Extend.ai to 95%+ across 10+ doc types required iterating on prompts, tuning edge cases, and building eval pipelines.

04
Normalizing messy pricing data

Every vendor names products differently. “Seats” become “licenses,” bundles hide real costs, and line items change between customers. AI can read the text — but comparing prices across vendors means building an intelligence layer that actually understands what the data means.

Coverage tiers — from full SKU data to negotiation-only insights

13
Reflection

What I would do differently

  • Be honest about what the data can and can’t tell you. We got Extend.ai to 95%+ accuracy across 10+ document types — but that didn’t happen overnight. Accuracy varied wildly by contract type and format, and the eval cycles were longer than we anticipated. We were a small team, so starting with human-in-the-loop was the right call — it let us ship while we built confidence in the automation. What I’d change is surfacing that investment earlier so the team could plan around it from the start.
  • Lower the first step, sooner. Our strategy was “every upload makes the product smarter” — but uploading a contract is a high-friction ask. Buyer Guide was already driving strong SEO traffic to pricing pages. I would have invested earlier in a low-friction price estimate — even without a document — to widen the funnel. The tradeoff: those users don’t feed the data flywheel. That tension needed to be confronted in strategy, not discovered in conversion metrics.
  • Launch fewer suppliers, better. The principle was “big rocks, not gravel” — top 200 suppliers where data was deepest. But we still returned something for every upload, even when data was thin. The negotiation-insights-only experience drove below-3-star ratings and diluted trust. I’d have launched a tighter set and expanded only as coverage earned it.
